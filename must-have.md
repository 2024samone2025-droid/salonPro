Core must-haves (your MVP)

Appointment booking & calendar — the heart of everything. Staff calendar views (day/week/per-stylist), drag-and-drop rescheduling, buffer times between appointments, recurring appointments, and double-booking prevention.
Online self-booking for clients — a booking page or widget clients can use 24/7 without calling. This is the #1 reason salons switch to software.
Client management (CRM) — profiles with contact info, visit history, service history, notes (hair formulas, color codes, allergies, preferences), and photos of past work.
Service catalog — services with duration, price, category, and which staff can perform them. Support variable pricing per stylist seniority.
Staff management — schedules, working hours, time off, services they offer, and commission rates.
Automated reminders — SMS/email/WhatsApp reminders to cut no-shows. This alone sells the product.
Payments & checkout (POS) — take payments, split payments, tips, deposits/prepayments to reduce no-shows, refunds, and receipts.
Basic reporting — daily revenue, appointments per staff, top services, no-show rates.

Strongly expected (clients will ask for these fast)

No-show & cancellation policies — cancellation windows, no-show fees, deposit enforcement.
Inventory management — track retail products and back-bar supplies, low-stock alerts, product sales at checkout.
Staff commissions & payroll reports — calculate earnings per stylist (commission %, flat rate, or hybrid).
Waitlist — auto-fill cancelled slots from a waitlist.
Packages, memberships & gift cards — prepaid bundles (e.g., 10 blowouts), monthly memberships, digital gift cards. Big revenue driver for salons.
Loyalty/rewards program — points per visit or spend.
Marketing tools — email/SMS campaigns, "we miss you" win-back messages for lapsed clients, birthday messages.
Multi-location support — even if you launch single-location, architect for this early. Retrofitting is painful.
Roles & permissions — owner vs. manager vs. receptionist vs. stylist access levels.

Differentiators / nice-to-haves

Review collection (auto-request Google reviews after visits)
Client mobile app or PWA
Consultation/intake forms (especially for color services, lash, brows, med-spa)
Before/after photo galleries per client
Resource management (rooms, chairs, equipment — so two services needing the same room can't overlap)
Walk-in queue management
Tip distribution and pooling
Online store for retail products
Analytics dashboards (client retention rate, average ticket, rebooking rate, utilization per chair)
Booth/chair rental management (many salons rent chairs to independent stylists)
Integrations: Google Calendar, Instagram/Facebook booking buttons, accounting software, payment processors

Non-functional things people forget

Mobile-first design — stylists live on their phones between clients.
Offline tolerance / fast loading — salon wifi is often bad.
Data import — a way to migrate clients from spreadsheets or competitors (Fresha, Booksy, Vagaro), or no one will switch to you.
Timezone handling, SMS costs, and local payment methods — especially relevant if you're targeting Rwanda/East Africa, where mobile money (MTN MoMo, Airtel Money) support could be a huge differentiator over Western competitors, and WhatsApp reminders matter more than SMS/email.
Subscription billing for your SaaS — plans, trials, usage limits.
Security & privacy — client data protection, audit logs.
